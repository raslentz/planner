A 2x2x2 Planner Problem

The economy consists of 2 individuals, \( A \) and \( B \), and two firms, \( X \) and \( Y\). Each individual takes utility from a combination of consumption, \( c \), and leisure, \( \ell \). Each individual is endowed with one unit of time which can be used toward leisure (either own or that of the other individual) and labor supply to the firms. Specifically, if individual \( A \) takes leisure \( \ell_A\), then the time \(1-\ell_A\) is available to be allocated to either the firms or to \(B\)'s leisure. The utility functions are given by, \begin{align*} u_A(c,\ell) & = \alpha c +\ell \\\\ u_B(c,\ell) & = c\ell. \end{align*} Firms \(X\) and \(Y\) take labor from the individuals and use it to produce the consumption good.
